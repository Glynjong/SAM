Self-Portrait (No More Tears, Mr. Lee)
2009

Jason Wee

Plastic shampoo bottle caps
380 x 324 x 30 cm
Singapore Art Museum collection

Jason Wee was born in Singapore in 1978 and graduated from the Parsons School of Design, New York with a Masters in Fine Arts in Photography.

Mr Lee Kuan Yew was one of Singapore’s founding fathers. He cried on television in 1965 when Singapore was separated from Malaysia! It was a tough and challenging time for our country then - won’t you agree it was a historical moment for our country that we must remember?

Self-Portrait (No More Tears, Mr. Lee) is made out of 8,000 plastic shampoo bottle caps placed individually on an angled platform called a pedestal. Using a combination of opened and closed bottle caps placed in a grid formation, the portrait of a person is created when the bottle caps are lit and viewed from a certain angle. The shampoo bottle caps are like pixels, which form a larger image. In this way, it represents the many pieces of historical figures like Mr Lee, in which their biographies and histories have many layers and may be interpreted in many ways.

Just like each bottle cap is an important piece in ensuring the shape of the artwork, it also reminds us that every little thing we do and every person in the world is important. Each piece helps to create and shape the character and image portrayed!

The title of this artwork comes from refers to the well-known shampoo tagline from a well-known brand. Guess which shampoo brand it is from!
